Line Following Robot

The Line-Following Robot is a collaborative engineering project that I worked on with my team to design and build a functional autonomous system capable of following a predefined path using infrared sensors and programmed control logic.

Additional sensors were integrated into the robot to expand its practical applications, including potential use in areas such as agricultural monitoring and automation — allowing the robot to detect environmental conditions beyond simple path tracking.

The project required combining hardware assembly, programming logic in C++, and systematic testing to ensure reliable and consistent performance across different track configurations.

Through this experience, I gained hands-on knowledge in robotics, embedded systems programming, sensor integration, and the importance of iterative testing and teamwork when solving real-world engineering problems.

// ACHIEVEMENT // 2026
NUS–SYNAPXE–IMDA AI Innovation Challenge
National University of Singapore

Our team identified Singapore's rapidly ageing population as a pressing challenge, and chose to focus specifically on elderly individuals living with dementia — a group that faces significant daily difficulties that are often managed by overstretched caregivers.

We developed a Telegram bot powered by AI that goes beyond passive reminders. Unlike conventional apps, our bot actively prompts users at scheduled intervals to check in on their well-being and medication intake, reducing the risk of missed doses and undetected health changes.

The bot also functions as an interactive assistant — elderly users can ask it questions and receive accurate, easy-to-understand responses tailored to their needs. This two-way communication was a key design priority, as it reduces the isolation many dementia patients experience.

Crucially, the system includes an alert mechanism that notifies caregivers in real time when the AI detects a situation it cannot handle — ensuring that human support is always available when it matters most.

This project reflects my belief that technology should be designed with empathy, and that engineering solutions are most meaningful when they directly improve the lives of vulnerable people.
